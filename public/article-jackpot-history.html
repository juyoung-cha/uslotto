The Largest Jackpots in US History and How They Were Won

Category: Lifestyle & History | Published: June 22, 2026

Over the last decade, US lotteries have reached record-breaking figures, with jackpots routinely crossing the billion-dollar mark. This rise is not accidental; changes to the lottery matrices in 2015 and 2017 made the odds of winning the jackpot harder, resulting in more rollovers and larger prizes. Let's look at the largest lottery jackpots in US history and the stories behind them.

1. The Record-Breaker: $2.04 Billion Powerball

On November 7, 2022, the Powerball jackpot reached **$2.04 Billion**, the largest jackpot in global history. The drawing was delayed by several hours due to a technical issue in one state's lottery processing system, adding to the drama. A single ticket purchased in Altadena, California, won the prize. The winner, Edwin Castro, chose the cash lump sum option of **$997.6 Million** and claimed his prize through a representative several months later.

2. The Three-Ticket Split: $1.586 Billion Powerball

Until 2022, the benchmark for lottery jackpots was the **$1.586 Billion** Powerball drawing on January 13, 2016. This drawing saw record-breaking ticket sales nationwide. Three winning tickets split the jackpot, purchased in California, Florida, and Tennessee. Each winning ticket was worth **$528.8 Million** (annuity value) or **$327.8 Million** (cash value):

  • Tennessee Winners: John and Lisa Robinson claimed their prize immediately and appeared on national television before consulting financial advisors.
  • Florida Winners: David Kaltschmidt and Maureen Smith claimed their prize through a trust a month later and chose to live quietly in their original home.
  • California Winners: Marvin and Mae Acosta waited six months to claim their prize, using that time to assemble a team of attorneys and financial planners.

3. The Anonymous Record: $1.537 Billion Mega Millions

On October 23, 2018, a Mega Millions jackpot reached **$1.537 Billion**. Unlike the 2016 Powerball jackpot, this prize was won by a single ticket purchased in South Carolina. South Carolina is one of the few states that allow lottery winners to remain completely anonymous. The winner waited until March 2019 to claim the prize, choosing the cash option of **$877.8 Million** and keeping their identity entirely private.

4. The Florida Mega Ball: $1.602 Billion Mega Millions

On August 8, 2023, a Mega Millions jackpot reached **$1.602 Billion** after 31 consecutive draws without a winner. A single ticket purchased at a Publix supermarket in Neptune Beach, Florida, won the jackpot. The winner claimed the prize through an LLC ("Saltines Holdings, LLC") to maintain their privacy under Florida's public record laws.

Conclusion: What These Jackpots Teach Us

These massive drawings show that when lottery jackpots grow, ticket sales increase exponentially. During billion-dollar runs, the volume of tickets purchased covers up to 80% of all possible number combinations, making a winner highly likely. While the odds of winning remain the same, these stories show that careful planning, patience, and choosing anonymity are key steps for any future winner.
