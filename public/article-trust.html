How to Claim Anonymously: Blind Trusts & Legal Entities

Category: Privacy & Law | Published: June 22, 2026

Winning the lottery is a dream, but the sudden publicity can quickly turn into a nightmare. In many states, lottery laws require the winner's name, city of residence, and prize amount to be made public. This transparency exists to show the public that the lottery is honest. However, it can expose winners to scammers, aggressive sales representatives, and unwanted attention. Here is how you can protect your identity.

1. States That Allow Direct Anonymity

A few U.S. states allow lottery winners to remain completely anonymous without setting up complex legal structures. These states include Arizona, Delaware, Georgia, Kansas, Maryland, Michigan, Mississippi, Montana, New Jersey, North Dakota, Ohio, South Carolina, Texas, Virginia, West Virginia, and Wyoming. If you bought your ticket in one of these states, you can simply request anonymity when you claim your prize.

2. The "Double Trust" Structure

If you live in a state that does not allow direct anonymity, you can often claim the prize through a trust. This is a common legal technique used by wealth managers. The typical structure involves two trusts:

  • 1. The Claiming Trust: You establish a revocable trust with a neutral name (e.g., "The Blue Sky Trust"). You name a trusted attorney or corporate trustee as the trustee who will physically sign the back of the ticket and submit it to the lottery commission. The trust's name becomes public, but your personal name does not.
  • 2. The Receiving Trust: The claiming trust then transfers the lottery money to a second, private trust (often called a "blind trust" or family trust) where you are the beneficiary. This keeps your financial affairs private and separate from the public claiming record.

3. Using a Limited Liability Company (LLC)

In some states, you can form an LLC to claim a lottery jackpot. Just like a trust, the LLC's representative claims the prize, and the LLC's name is released to the public. If the LLC is registered in a state that does not require disclosure of the owners' names (like Wyoming, Delaware, or New Mexico), your privacy is secure. Once the funds are in the LLC's bank account, they can be distributed to you privately.

Important Steps to Take Before Claiming

If you plan to claim your prize anonymously, do not rush. Take these key steps:

  1. Do not sign the ticket yet: If you sign your personal name on the back of the ticket, you may disqualify yourself from claiming it through a trust or LLC. Consult a lottery lawyer first to see how the ticket should be signed.
  2. Secure the ticket: Keep the ticket in a safe place, like a bank safety deposit box, while your legal team sets up the trust or LLC.
  3. Maintain strict confidentiality: Tell only your spouse and your legal counsel. The fewer people who know, the less likely the information is to leak before your trust is established.

Conclusion: Work with Professionals

Protecting your identity when winning a jackpot requires precise planning and execution. Working with an experienced estate planning attorney is essential to ensure that your trusts and LLCs comply with your state's lottery laws while providing the maximum possible privacy and tax protection.
