Mega Millions vs. Powerball: Which Has Better Odds and Value?

Category: Strategy Guide | Published: June 22, 2026

For lottery enthusiasts in the United States, Powerball and Mega Millions are the two main multi-state lottery games. Both cost $2 per ticket, draw three times a week, and offer jackpots that frequently exceed hundreds of millions of dollars. However, their rules, odds, and value propositions are slightly different. Let's compare them head-to-head to see where you should focus your play.

1. The Matrix and Jackpot Odds

The primary difference between the two games is the number of balls in the drawings, which affects the probability of winning the jackpot:

Feature Powerball Mega Millions
White Ball Pool Choose 5 from 69 Choose 5 from 70
Special Ball Pool Choose 1 from 26 Choose 1 from 25
Jackpot Odds 1 in 292,201,338 1 in 302,575,350
Overall Odds of Any Prize 1 in 24.87 1 in 24.00

Powerball gives you a slightly better chance of winning the jackpot (1 in 292 Million vs. 1 in 302 Million in Mega Millions). However, Mega Millions has slightly better overall odds of winning any prize (1 in 24.00 vs. 1 in 24.87 for Powerball).

2. Ticket Features: Power Play vs. Megaplier

Both lotteries offer a $1 add-on feature to multiply non-jackpot winnings. However, the way they draw and apply these multipliers differs:

  • Powerball Power Play: Uses a pool of 2x, 3x, 4x, 5x, and 10x multipliers. The 10x multiplier is only active when the jackpot is $150 Million or less. The second-tier prize (Match 5 white balls) is capped at a maximum of **$2 Million**, regardless of the multiplier.
  • Mega Millions Megaplier: Uses a pool of 2x, 3x, 4x, and 5x Megapliers. Unlike Powerball, there is no jackpot limit for higher multipliers, and the second-tier prize (Match 5 white balls) is multiplied by the Megaplier drawn, meaning you can win up to **$5 Million** if the 5x Megaplier is drawn.

In terms of multiplier value, **Mega Millions Megaplier** is superior for the second-tier prize, as it can multiply the $1 Million Match 5 payout up to $5 Million, whereas Powerball caps it at $2 Million.

3. Jackpot Starting Ratios and Payouts

Historically, both games started their jackpots at $40 Million, which grew by a minimum of $5 Million per rollover. Since the 2020 pandemic, starting jackpots are determined by ticket sales and interest rates. Generally, Powerball tends to build momentum slightly faster due to its larger network of participating states, but both games routinely reach massive figures.

Conclusion: Which Should You Play?

From a mathematical perspective, if you are playing specifically to win the jackpot, **Powerball** has a minor advantage because the odds are slightly better (by about 3%). If you want to play for high-tier secondary prizes, **Mega Millions** with the Megaplier option offers better potential payouts (up to $5 Million for Match 5). Ultimately, many players choose to play both or focus their purchases on whichever game has the larger jackpot at the time.
