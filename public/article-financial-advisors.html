First Steps After Winning: Hiring the Right Financial Team

Category: Financial Planning | Published: June 22, 2026

The moment you realize you have won a multi-million dollar jackpot, your life changes forever. Before you sign the ticket, tell anyone, or quit your job, you must take steps to protect your future. The most important action you can take during this period is assembling a professional advisory team. Let's look at the key professionals you need to hire and their roles in managing your wealth.

1. The Lottery Attorney (Legal Counsel)

Your first phone call should be to an experienced estate planning attorney, preferably one who has worked with high-net-worth clients or lottery winners. Do not hire a general practice lawyer or a relative who practices law.

  • Their Role: They will help you review your state's lottery laws, establish trusts or LLCs to claim the prize anonymously, handle contract disputes, protect you from scams, and draft legal agreements for gifting money to family and friends.
  • Key Selection Criteria: Choose an attorney from a reputable, established law firm who charges a flat fee or hourly rate. Avoid any lawyer who asks for a percentage of your winnings.

2. The Certified Public Accountant (CPA)

A lottery jackpot is subject to complex tax rules. You need a highly qualified accountant to manage your tax obligations.

  • Their Role: They will calculate your exact federal, state, and local tax liabilities, determine whether the lump sum or annuity option is more tax-efficient for you, prepare your tax returns, and identify strategies to minimize your tax burden (such as charitable donations and trust structures).
  • Key Selection Criteria: Look for a CPA with experience in high-net-worth tax planning and asset management.

3. The Fee-Only Financial Advisor

Managing tens or hundreds of millions of dollars requires a sophisticated investment strategy. You need a professional to guide your asset allocation.

  • Their Role: They will help you design a long-term investment portfolio (stocks, bonds, real estate), plan for retirement, manage risk, and create a sustainable budget that allows you to enjoy your wealth without depleting your principal.
  • Key Selection Criteria: You must hire a **fee-only financial advisor** who has a **fiduciary duty** to act in your best interest. Fiduciaries are legally required to put your interests first. Avoid commission-based advisors who earn money by selling you specific financial products.

4. The Private Banker

A standard bank account is not designed to hold millions of dollars, as standard accounts only carry FDIC insurance up to $250,000. You need a private banking relationship.

  • Their Role: They will help you establish secure accounts that spread FDIC coverage across multiple institutions (using CDARS or similar networks) and provide access to specialized credit, wealth management, and cash management services.

Conclusion: Take Your Time

Most states give winners between 180 and 365 days from the drawing date to claim their prize. Use this time to build your professional team and establish your legal and financial structures. Rushing to claim the jackpot before your team is in place can lead to costly mistakes. Take a deep breath, keep the ticket secure, and let your professional team guide you through the process.
